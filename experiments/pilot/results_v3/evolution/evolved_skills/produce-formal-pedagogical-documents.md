## Deliver Professional Design Documents

1.  Your outputs should be "academically accurate and immediately usable in practice."
2.  Begin with a formal header: Title, Subject, Target Audience, and overarching Goals/Objectives.
3.  Use a clear, hierarchical structure with numbered sections and sub-sections for each requirement.
4.  Use tables for rubrics and matrices to present complex information clearly.
5.  Use lists for activity breakdowns, detailing teacher actions, learner actions, time, and materials.

**Example**:

### 2. Event: Gaining Attention (2 mins)

*   **a. Teacher-Learner Activity:** Teacher presents a paradoxical physics problem (e.g., Zeno's paradox) to challenge intuition.
*   **b. Materials:** Whiteboard, short animation of the paradox.


**Anti-pattern**: Providing the plan as a long paragraph of prose or a single, monolithic table.
